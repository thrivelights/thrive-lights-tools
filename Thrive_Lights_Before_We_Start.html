Organization Information

1

Staff and Facilitators

Facilitators are the people who will work directly with families using the Thrive Lights tool. They are the heart of the program.
1.1
Our organization has identified at least one staff member or committed volunteer to serve as a Thrive Lights facilitator.
1.2
Our identified facilitator(s) have a background in social work, psychology, case management, or a related field.
1.3
Our identified facilitator(s) have at least 2 years of experience working with families or individuals on goal-setting and life circumstances.
1.4
Our facilitator(s) have availability to complete the required 20 hours of Thrive Lights training.
1.5
We have a signed agreement (MOU, volunteer agreement, or employment contract) in place with our facilitator(s).
Section 1 Notes: Anything else about your staffing situation we should know?
2

Adaptation Team

The Adaptation Team is a small group who will help localize the Thrive Lights assessment to fit your community. This is about making the tool meaningful and culturally relevant for the families you serve.
2.1
Our organization has an active group or committee that can serve as an Adaptation Team (program manager, community leader, subject matter expert, etc.).
2.2
The Adaptation Team members have time in their schedules to review the assessment, discuss indicators, and attend focus groups.
2.3
The Adaptation Team includes at least one person with deep knowledge of the community you are serving.
2.4
We have a signed agreement or letter of participation from Adaptation Team members.
Section 2 Notes: Who makes up your Adaptation Team, or who are you considering?
3

Training Readiness

The 20-hour training is required for all facilitators and Adaptation Team members. It takes place over Zoom and covers the full Thrive Lights methodology.
3.1
Our facilitator(s) and Adaptation Team members are aware that 20 hours of training is required and have committed to completing it.
3.2
We have identified a time of day and days of the week when most participants can attend training sessions.
3.3
All training participants have reliable internet access and a device for joining Zoom sessions.
3.4
Our organization has a plan for how training recordings and materials will be stored, shared with staff who may join later, and used to train new facilitators as the team grows (train the trainer).
Section 3 Notes: Any scheduling constraints or barriers to training we should plan around?
4

Community Engagement (Focus Groups)

Focus groups bring together community members to review the Thrive Lights indicators before they are used. This step makes sure the assessment reflects real life for the families in your program.
4.1
Our organization can identify approximately 15 community representatives to participate in three separate focus groups of about 5 participants each.
4.2
We have the capacity to organize and facilitate these focus groups (either in person or online).
4.3
We have considered whether we will offer incentives to focus group participants (not required, but encouraged).
4.4
The Adaptation Team is prepared to take notes, analyze feedback, and communicate findings.
Section 4 Notes: Any context about your community or focus group logistics?
5

Field Testing

Field testing means piloting the adapted assessment with a small group of community members before rolling it out fully. This is how you catch any issues before they affect families at scale.
5.1
Our organization can identify approximately 5 community representatives for a field testing session.
5.2
We have access to at least two tablets or devices for the field testing session.
5.3
We have a plan for who will lead the field testing session and how feedback will be recorded.
5.4
We have considered whether we will offer incentives to field testing participants (not required, but encouraged).
Section 5 Notes: Any context about field testing readiness or device availability?
6

Organizational Commitment

This section is about leadership buy-in and operational structure. Even when all the right people are in place, implementation struggles when the organizational foundation is not solid. This is where Thrive Lights can offer the most targeted support.
6.1
Our organization's leadership is aware of and supportive of implementing Thrive Lights.
6.2
We have reviewed the budget and have a sense of what resources (staff time, technology, incentives) are needed.
6.3
We have or are prepared to build a work plan with clear timelines and responsible parties.
6.4
We have a system for regular internal communication about Thrive Lights progress (weekly or bi-weekly check-ins, shared documents, etc.).
6.5
We have identified who the main point of contact will be for coordinating with Thrive Lights staff.
